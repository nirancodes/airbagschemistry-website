The Chemistry Behind Car Airbags

Introduction and History

Fun Fact: The first airbag design was not commercially used because the spring could not accurately determine if the car was in a crash, or if it just drove over a pothole. Plus, the compressed air was not quick enough to fill the airbag to protect the passenger. There were also special arrangements to store the compressed air - as a tank in the trunk of the car. The idea of using chemical explosives like sodium azide instead of compressed air was also one of Breed’s developments.
